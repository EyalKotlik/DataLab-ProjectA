Technion · DataLab · Section B

Dense–sparse retrieval over a
synthetic Wikipedia corpus

Task: for each query, return a ranked list of page_ids from 27,074 entries. Evaluation: mean NDCG@10 over 29 public queries.

Final system: mean NDCG@10 = 0.4338  ·  runtime ~26 s (60 s budget)  ·  fixed all-MiniLM-L6 encoder

[Speaker A] & [Speaker B]

[A]: This is our Section B submission: a retrieval system over a synthetic Wikipedia corpus. Given a query, it returns a ranked list of page IDs from twenty-seven thousand entries, evaluated by mean NDCG at ten. The final system reaches 0.4338, within the sixty-second budget, using the fixed MiniLM encoder.

Corpus and query characteristics

  • 27,074 synthetic pages; entities and dates are deliberately fictionalized (years shifted by ~160 yrs)
  • Relevant ("answer") pages are short — ~50–280 content words; distractor pages are long (median ~1,218 words)
  • 29 queries, mean 2.0 relevant pages each
  • Two systematic query–document gaps: temporal ("1820s" vs. "1826") and synonym paraphrase (a distinctive query term is absent from the answer page)
[B]: The corpus is deliberately hard: fictionalized entities, years shifted by a century. Relevant pages are short — fifty to two-eighty words — while distractors exceed twelve hundred. There are twenty-nine queries, about two relevant pages each, with temporal and synonym-paraphrase gaps.

Diagnosis: recall is sufficient, ranking is not

  • Pure BM25 over the full corpus: NDCG@10 = 0.3191
  • The relevant page is reliably retrievable: recall@500 = 1.00, recall@50 = 0.90
  • But only recall@10 = 0.66 — a third of answers miss the top 10
  • Implication: the task is reranking within a candidate set, not expanding recall

BM25 recall@k on the 29-query set; the top-10 operating depth is highlighted.

[A]: We start with a diagnosis. Pure BM25 scores 0.32, and its recall is high: the answer is always in the top five hundred, and in the top fifty ninety percent of the time. But only sixty-six percent reach the top ten. So the bottleneck is reranking within a candidate set, not retrieving more candidates.

Offline stages: chunk · embed · index

chunk
One lead unit per page = title + content (entry_text). Body windows are indexed but excluded from scoring.
embed
all-MiniLM-L6-v2, 384-d, mean-pooled, L2-normalized — fixed by the assignment.
index
148,214 vectors (numpy / faiss) + a BM25 index over 485,286 terms.

Lead unit vs. body-chunk max-pooling (29-query set): 0.4338 vs. 0.4115. Long pages dominate max-pooling, so body chunks are excluded from the default.

[B]: The offline pipeline has three stages. Chunk: one lead unit per page — title plus content; body windows are indexed but excluded from scoring. Embed: the fixed MiniLM-L6 model, 384-dimensional, L2-normalized. Index: roughly a hundred-fifty-thousand vectors plus a BM25 index over four-hundred-eighty-five-thousand terms.

Retrieval: z-score fusion of dense and sparse (zfuse)

Cq  =  ⋃ top-300 BM25(q)# per-query candidate union
sdense(p)  =  cos(q, leadp) − β · log(wordsp)
score(p)  =  w · zCq(sdense)  +  (1−w) · zCq(sBM25)

w = 0.8 (dense weight)  ·  β = 0.15 (length prior)  ·  |Cq| = 300  ·  z = z-score over the candidate set

  • Candidate restriction removes globally-similar long-page distractors
  • The length prior demotes long pages; BM25 re-anchors the rare exact matches (names, numbers) that a strong prior would otherwise bury
[A]: Retrieval fuses the two signals. Candidates are the union of the BM25 top three hundred per query. Each candidate's dense score is cosine similarity minus beta times log word-count. We then z-score-normalize the dense and BM25 scores over the candidate set and combine them with dense weight 0.8. BM25 re-anchors the exact matches the prior would bury.

Component ablation: incremental contribution

Previous default 0.2527 → pure BM25 0.3191 → dense over BM25 pool 0.3425 → + z-score fusion 0.3911 → + length prior (zfuse) 0.4338. Same artifacts; retrieval logic only.

[B]: This ablation isolates each component. The previous default scored 0.25; pure BM25, 0.32; restricting dense scoring to the BM25 pool, 0.34; adding z-score fusion, 0.39; and the length prior gives the final 0.4338 — same artifacts, retrieval logic only.

Length-prior sensitivity (β)

Prior alone: optimum near β=0.05 (0.4053), then monotone decline as exact matches are over-penalized. Fused with BM25: optimum at β=0.15 (0.4332), because BM25 preserves the exact-match signal.

[A]: The prior is sensitive to beta. Applied alone, it peaks near beta 0.05 then declines, over-penalizing pages with rare exact matches. Fused with BM25, the optimum shifts to 0.15, because BM25 preserves the exact-match signal — the two components are complementary.

Rejected alternatives (all measured ≤ baseline)

Body-chunk pooling 0.4115 · title-blended vector 0.4044 · sentence-granularity 0.2678 · global-dense union 0.1941. Widening the candidate pool (300→1500) also degraded results monotonically.

[B]: We also rejected several alternatives, each at or below baseline: body-chunk pooling, a title-blended vector, sentence-level matching, and injecting global dense neighbors — the worst of the set. Widening the candidate pool also degraded results.

Parameter grid and ceiling analysis

  • Error analysis: 93/100 relevant pages already lie in the candidate pool — most failures are in-pool ranking errors, plus 7 out-of-pool pages across 4 queries
  • The (dense_w × β) grid attains its global maximum at the operating point (0.8, 0.15)
  • Closing the residual gap requires a stronger ranker (cross-encoder), disallowed under the fixed-model constraint

mean NDCG@10 over the (dense_w × β) grid

[A]: On the ceiling: error analysis shows ninety-three of a hundred relevant pages are already in the pool, so failures are mostly in-pool ranking errors. Our operating point is the global maximum of the dense-weight–beta grid. Closing the remaining gap would require a cross-encoder, which the fixed-model constraint disallows.

Summary

  • Method. BM25 candidate generation → lead-chunk dense scoring with a log-length prior → z-score fusion of the dense and sparse signals.
  • Result. mean NDCG@10 = 0.4338 on the 29 public queries (+0.18 over the previous default), within the 60 s budget (~26 s).
  • Validation. each component justified by ablation; the operating point is the grid maximum; the achievable ceiling is bounded by the fixed encoder.
